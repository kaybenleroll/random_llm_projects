# Research: Small-Form-Factor Android Tablets in Dublin
**Date:** Wednesday 22 April 2026

This report evaluates small-form-factor (7-9 inch) Android tablets currently in stock at CeX stores across Dublin. I have divided these into **Recommended** and **Not Recommended** categories based on their age, performance, and software support.

---

## ℹ️ Understanding CeX Grading & Pricing
CeX uses a strict grading system that significantly affects the value and price of the devices. Prices listed below are ranges depending on the grade.
*   **Grade A:** Mint condition, looks brand new. Includes the original box and all original accessories (charger, cable). Highest price, best if purchasing as a gift.
*   **Grade B:** Good condition. May have light cosmetic scratches or marks on the casing or screen (not visible when the screen is on). Comes with a compatible charger (box not guaranteed). **The sweet spot for value.**
*   **Grade C:** Working condition but with significant cosmetic wear, deep scratches, or minor dents. Comes with a compatible charger. Cheapest option, good for a "beater" tablet or for kids where cosmetic damage is expected.

## ❌ Not Recommended (Avoid)
While these are cheap and currently in stock in Dublin, they are likely to be frustrating to use in 2026.

### 1. Samsung Galaxy Tab A 8.0 (2017)
*   **Release Year:** 2017
*   **Price:** ~€70 (Grade B) | ~€60 (Grade C)
*   **Dublin Stock:** Liffey Valley (Grade B), George's St (Grade C).
*   **Reason to Avoid:** This device is nearly 9 years old. The Snapdragon 425 processor and 2GB of RAM will struggle with modern versions of Chrome and media apps. Battery health on these older units is also likely to be poor.

### 2. Samsung Galaxy Tab E 9.6
*   **Release Year:** 2015
*   **Price:** ~€60 (Grade B) | ~€50 (Grade C)
*   **Dublin Stock:** Nutgrove, Ilac Centre.
*   **Reason to Avoid:** Extremely outdated software (Android 5.1). Many apps will refuse to install or run entirely. The screen resolution (800 x 1280) on a 9.6" panel is very pixelated by modern standards.

### 3. Samsung Galaxy Tab 4 7.0
*   **Release Year:** 2014
*   **Price:** ~€45 (Grade B/C)
*   **Dublin Stock:** Rathmines.
*   **Reason to Avoid:** Essentially a collector's item or for parts only at this stage. It lacks the processing power to run a modern web browser effectively.

### 4. Generic 7-inch Tablets (e.g., Alba, Alcatel 1T)
*   **Release Year:** Various (usually 2018-2020)
*   **Price:** ~€30 - €45 (Grade B/C)
*   **Dublin Stock:** George's St, Liffey Valley.
*   **Reason to Avoid:** These often use very low-quality "TN" screens with poor viewing angles (the colors shift if you don't look at it straight on) and have very limited storage that fills up almost immediately.

### **Summary of Recommendation:**
If your budget allows, the **Samsung Galaxy Tab A9 (2023)** is the clear winner for performance and longevity. If you want something under €100, the **Lenovo Tab M8 (2023)** or **Samsung A7 Lite (2021)** are your best bets. For the best value-to-condition ratio, **Grade B** is highly recommended as it offers significant savings over Grade A while ensuring the device is fully functional and presentable. Avoid anything released before 2020 unless you have a very specific, lightweight use case.
