SILO SYSTEM: PLAYER’S SURVIVAL GUIDE

“We do not know why we are here. We do not know who built the Silo. We only know that the world outside is dead, and the Silo is life.”

Welcome to the Silo. This is your entire universe. It is a massive, subterranean cylinder of concrete and steel, extending 144 levels deep into the earth. There are no elevators. A single, colossal spiral staircase acts as the central artery for thousands of citizens.

You have never seen the sun, felt rain, or breathed un-recycled air. You wear patched clothes woven from hydroponic cotton. You eat processed soy, algae paste, and if you are very lucky, a morsel of rabbit meat. You work a grueling 12-hour shift, turning valves, hauling freight, or balancing ledgers to ensure the survival of the collective.

The Silo is a pressure cooker of scarcity, bureaucracy, and paranoia. Survival means keeping your head down, doing your job, and never, ever questioning the nature of your confinement.

THE THREE ABSOLUTE TRUTHS

Every child in the Silo is taught these three truths from the moment they can speak. To question them is not just illegal; it is treated as a dangerous mental illness.

1. The Outside is Death

The world above is a toxic, blasted wasteland. The only window to this world is the “Viewport” in the Level 1 Cafeteria—a massive screen hooked to an external camera. It shows a landscape of ruined, dead hills beneath a grey, swirling sky, littered with the rotting bodies of those who came before. The air outside will melt your lungs in minutes.

2. The Pact is Law

There is no constitution, no bill of rights, and no forgiving judge. There is only The Pact. It is a massive, ancient legal text that dictates every aspect of your life. It dictates how many children can be born, the required thread count of a mechanic’s coveralls, and the exact punishment for stealing a ration of water. Judicial clerks enforce The Pact with terrifying, absolute authority.

3. The Cleaning is Our Burden

The ultimate punishment for any major crime—or for uttering the forbidden words, “I want to go outside”—is The Cleaning. The condemned is fitted with a hazmat suit and sent out the airlock to die. But before they die, as the toxic air eats through their suit, they are compelled by some profound, tragic realization to take a wool pad and clean the lenses of the Silo’s external cameras. They wipe away the grime so those inside can clearly see the wasteland, reminding everyone why they must stay inside.

THE WORLD YOU KNOW (THE THREE SECTORS)

You likely rarely leave a tight three-level radius around your home and workplace. Traveling more than ten levels is an exhausting journey requiring packed rations and canteens of water. Because of this, the Silo is fiercely divided by geography.

Up Top (Levels 1–48)

  • The Vibe: Clean air, bright halogen lights, white walls, and the smell of sterile floor wax.
  • The People: The elite. The Mayor, the Sheriff, the Judicial clerks, and the IT department live and work here. They speak with formal, crisp accents. They wear clean blues and whites. To the rest of the Silo, they are privileged, out-of-touch bureaucrats who don’t know what it takes to keep the lights on.
  • The Danger: Surveillance. Up Top, there are cameras everywhere. Everyone is watching everyone else. A misplaced word here can get you arrested by the Sheriff’s deputies before you even reach the stairs.

The Mids (Levels 49–96)

  • The Vibe: Humid, crowded, and smelling permanently of damp earth, fertilizer, and unwashed bodies.
  • The People: The working class. This sector houses the sprawling hydroponic farms, the Medical wards, the Nurseries, and the massive Supply depots. The people here are pragmatic, territorial, and fiercely loyal to their local levels. They wear practical greens and browns.
  • The Danger: Starvation and disease. If a crop blights or a water filter fails, the Mids suffer first and worst. Life here is a constant hustle, trading favors and “chits” (currency) to get by.

Down Deep (Levels 97–144)

  • The Vibe: Sweltering heat, the deafening roar of machinery, and the taste of metallic dust and crude oil in the air. Dimly lit by bare emergency bulbs.
  • The People: The “grease.” Mechanics, miners, and oil drillers. They are physically imposing, covered in permanent grease stains, and speak “Deep Talk”—a guttural slang that cuts through the noise of the generator. They resent the Up Top elites who reap the benefits of their backbreaking labor.
  • The Danger: Industrial death. A bursting steam pipe, a collapsed mine shaft, or a failing air scrubber are daily threats. The Down Deep is dangerous, but the people there know that without them, the entire Silo dies in the dark.

YOUR PLACE IN THE MACHINE (CHARACTER ROLES)

When you create your character, you aren’t an adventurer. You are a citizen with a job. Who are you in the machine?

  • The Porter: You are the postal service, the freight train, and the rumor mill. Porters run the stairs all day, carrying goods and messages. You have incredible stamina, calves of steel, and you know the layout of the Silo better than anyone. You also hear everything.
  • The Mechanic: You keep the beast breathing. Whether you work on the great Generator at Level 144, or fix the local air scrubbers in the Mids, you know how to jury-rig ancient technology with nothing but a wrench and scrap metal.
  • The Supply Clerk: You are the master of red tape. You know exactly how many lightbulbs are on Level 40 and who is hoarding water on Level 60. You survive by trading favors, manipulating the inventory ledgers, and knowing exactly who to bribe.
  • The Hydroponics Farmer: You grow the food. You spend your days under artificial suns, tending to algae vats and soy plants. You are tough, protective of your yields, and possess a rare knowledge of biology and chemistry.
  • The IT Tech / Medical Assistant: You belong to the educated class. You have access to the rare, functioning computers or the tightly controlled pharmaceutical cabinets. You have a slightly higher standard of living, but you are also much closer to the terrifying gaze of Judicial.

A FINAL WARNING

The Silo is not just a place; it is a living, breathing entity that wants to maintain the status quo at all costs.

As you go about your life, remember that Judicial commands a network of secret informants known as the “Friends of the Silo.” They do not wear uniforms. They are your neighbor, the bartender who serves your synthetic gin, or the doctor setting your broken arm.

Keep your head down. Do not ask about the “Before” time. Do not horde relics of the old world. Do not ask why the IT servers are locked behind steel doors.

Protect your family, do your job, and never look too long at the Viewport.

Welcome to the Silo.
