# Electoral System of Spain: D'Hondt and Closed Lists

Spain's electoral system for the Congress of Deputies is designed to provide proportional representation while maintaining a degree of stability and favoring larger parties through the use of provinces as electoral districts.

## 1. Core Mechanics

### Closed Party Lists
Voters do not pick individual candidates. They vote for a single party list. The political party determines the order of candidates, and voters cannot change that order (*"closed"*).

### Provincial Districts
Seats are allocated by province. Spain has 50 provinces (plus 2 autonomous cities). Smaller provinces are guaranteed a minimum of 2 seats, which leads to rural over-representation.

### The D'Hondt Method
To distribute seats within a province, the votes are divided using the D'Hondt formula. This system slightly favors the largest parties compared to other proportional methods.

---

## 2. Illustrated Example: Allocation of Seats

**Scenario:** A province with **5 seats** and four parties.

### Votes Received
*   **Party A:** 100,000
*   **Party B:** 75,000
*   **Party C:** 45,000
*   **Party D:** 15,000

### The D'Hondt Table
We divide the total votes for each party by 1, 2, 3, 4, and 5. The highest quotients across the entire table win the seats.

| Party | ÷ 1 | ÷ 2 | ÷ 3 | ÷ 4 | ÷ 5 |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Party A** | **100,000 (1st)** | **50,000 (3rd)** | 33,333 | 25,000 | 20,000 |
| **Party B** | **75,000 (2nd)** | **37,500 (4th)** | 25,000 | 18,750 | 15,000 |
| **Party C** | **45,000 (5th)** | 22,500 | 15,000 | 11,250 | 9,000 |
| **Party D** | 15,000 | 7,500 | 5,000 | 3,750 | 3,000 |

### Final Result
*   **Party A:** 2 seats
*   **Party B:** 2 seats
*   **Party C:** 1 seat
*   **Party D:** 0 seats

**Analysis:** Even though Party D got some votes, it failed to reach a high enough quotient to secure a seat. In small provinces with only 3 seats, Party C might have also received 0 seats despite getting over 15% of the vote.

---

## 3. Key Characteristics
*   **Party Power:** Since lists are closed, party leadership has total control over who gets into parliament.
*   **Bipartisan Bias:** The combination of small provincial districts and the D'Hondt method historically favored the two main parties (PP and PSOE).
*   **Geography:** Rural voters have significantly more power per person than urban voters due to the minimum seat guarantee for small provinces.
