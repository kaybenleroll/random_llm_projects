# Electoral System of Germany: Mixed-Member Proportional (MMP)

Germany uses a **Mixed-Member Proportional (MMP)** system, often called "Personalized Proportional Representation." It seeks to combine the benefits of local representation (like the UK) with overall national fairness (proportionality).

## 1. Core Mechanics

### The Two-Vote System
Every voter has two votes on a single ballot:
1.  **First Vote (*Erststimme*):** For a **local candidate** in your district. The winner is decided by First-Past-The-Post.
2.  **Second Vote (*Zweitstimme*):** For a **political party**. This is the most important vote because it determines the total number of seats a party gets in the Bundestag.

### Seat Allocation
*   **Direct Seats:** All winners of the "First Vote" automatically get a seat.
*   **List Seats:** If a party is entitled to 100 seats based on the "Second Vote" but only won 30 local districts, they are given 70 "list seats" from their pre-selected candidates to make up the total.

### 2024 Reform (The Cap)
Previously, the Bundestag could grow indefinitely to accommodate "overhang seats." A 2024 reform capped the size at **630 seats**. If a party wins more local seats than their proportional share allows, the local winners with the lowest percentage of the vote in their districts **do not get a seat**.

## 2. Illustrated Example

**Scenario:** A Parliament with **100 seats**.

### Vote Percentages (Second Vote)
*   **Party A:** 40% (Entitled to 40 seats)
*   **Party B:** 30% (Entitled to 30 seats)
*   **Party C:** 30% (Entitled to 30 seats)

### Local District Winners (First Vote)
*   **Party A:** Wins 45 local districts.
*   **Party B:** Wins 10 local districts.
*   **Party C:** Wins 5 local districts.

### Final Allocation (Under New 2024 Rules)
1.  **Party B:** Has 10 local winners + 20 from the list = **30 total**.
2.  **Party C:** Has 5 local winners + 25 from the list = **30 total**.
3.  **Party A:** They won 45 local districts, but are only entitled to **40 seats**. 
    *   The **5 local winners** with the lowest vote percentages in their specific districts **do not enter Parliament**.
    *   Total for Party A = **40**.

**Note:** In the old system, the Parliament would have grown to 115 seats to let all of Party A's winners in, and then given extra seats to Party B and C to restore proportionality.

---

## 3. Key Characteristics
*   **5% Threshold:** To prevent fragmentation (like in the Weimar Republic), a party must get at least 5% of the national vote to enter the Bundestag.
*   **Proportionality is King:** The Second Vote is the ultimate decider of power.
*   **Complexity:** The math behind the allocation is complex, but the result is generally seen as very fair.
